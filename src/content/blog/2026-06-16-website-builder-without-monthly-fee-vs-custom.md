"Is there a website builder without a monthly fee?" is one of the most common questions I get — and the honest answer is: not really, not for a proper business site. Wix, Squarespace, Shopify, and the rest are built around a subscription. The free tiers come with their branding, their subdomain, and hard limits; the moment you want your own domain and a professional look, you're on a monthly plan.

So the real question isn't "which builder is free" — it's "should I rent a website forever, or build one once and own it?" Here's the honest comparison.

## What you're actually paying for with a builder

A website builder's monthly fee (typically ₹400–2,500/month in India for a business plan) buys you convenience: drag-and-drop editing, hosting, templates, and support, with no developer involved. For some people that's exactly the right trade. But the fee is rent — stop paying and the site goes away, because you never owned it. You're also renting:

- **Their templates** — your site looks like thousands of others on the same platform.
- **Their limits** — you can only do what the builder allows. Custom features hit a wall.
- **Their performance** — builder sites carry heavy page weight, which can slow load times and hurt SEO.
- **Your data on their terms** — exporting and migrating away is deliberately awkward.

## What a one-time custom site costs instead

A custom-built website is a single payment — no subscription to me, no platform rent. From my [custom website cost guide](/blog/how-much-does-a-custom-website-cost-in-india), a business website runs **₹5,000–20,000** as a one-time build. You own the code, the design, and the hosting account. Most sites I build run on free hosting tiers (Netlify), so after the build your only recurring cost is the domain — roughly ₹800–1,500/year, registered in your name.

Real examples, all live: [Toondemy Preschool](/work/toondemy-preschool), [Aarti Jewellers](/work/aarti-jewellers) with live gold-and-silver prices, and the [SikhAid NGO](/work/sikhaid-ngo) donation platform — each a one-time build the client owns outright.

Within two to three years, a custom site has usually paid for itself versus the subscription — and from then on it's free to keep, while the builder keeps billing.

## When a builder is genuinely the right call

I'd rather be honest than sell you something you don't need. Use a website builder when:

- You need a simple site **live this week** and can't wait for a build.
- It's a **short-term** site — an event, a temporary campaign — you'll shut down in months.
- You want to **edit everything yourself** daily with zero technical help and no developer on call.
- You're **testing an idea** and don't yet know if the business will stick.

In those cases the subscription is buying you speed and self-service, and that's worth it.

## When a custom site wins

Go custom when the website is a long-term part of your business:

- You'll keep it running for **years** — the subscription math turns against renting fast.
- You want a site that **looks like yours**, not a template.
- You need **custom features** a builder can't do — live data, a specific booking flow, an integration.
- You care about **owning your code and data**, with the freedom to have any developer work on it later.
- You want the **best possible SEO and speed**, built lean rather than loaded with builder overhead.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is there a truly free website builder for a business?

Not for a professional site. Free tiers force a platform subdomain and branding and block custom domains. A usable business plan is a monthly subscription. The only way to avoid recurring fees entirely is a one-time custom build that you own.

### Is a custom website cheaper than Wix or Squarespace?

Over time, usually yes. A custom business site is ₹5,000–20,000 once; a builder is that much *every year or two* in subscription fees, indefinitely. The break-even is typically two to three years, after which the custom site costs nothing to keep.

### Can I edit a custom website myself like I can on Wix?

Yes — if that's a requirement, I build in a simple content management system or admin panel so you can update text and images yourself. It's one of the things that affects the quote, and we agree on it upfront.

### What happens to my Wix/Squarespace site if I stop paying?

It goes offline. You're renting, so the site and often your data disappear when the subscription ends. With a custom build you own everything, so it keeps running regardless.
